# The Supplier Landscape Matrix

> *A structured way to map vendors by capability, market presence, and risk — on a single page.*

## When to use

Use this framework whenever you are entering a new category, refreshing an existing supplier panel, or preparing for a multi-supplier negotiation. It is also useful as a pre-RFP filtering tool.

## The matrix

The Supplier Landscape Matrix maps every relevant vendor along three axes, then plots them on a 2x2 grid for executive consumption.

### Axis 1 — Capability (1 to 5)

How well does the supplier solve the specific problem you are buying for? Score each supplier from 1 (cannot solve) to 5 (best-in-class). Capability is *not* a measure of company size; a small specialist can score 5 while a global generalist scores 3.

### Axis 2 — Market presence (1 to 5)

How much of the addressable market does this supplier already serve? This is a proxy for credibility, reference availability, and operational scale. Use share of wallet within your peer set as the input where available, otherwise use revenue.

### Axis 3 — Risk (1 to 5)

What is the probability and severity of supply disruption from this vendor? Combine financial health, geographic concentration, single-points-of-failure, and any current geopolitical exposure. **Lower is better.**

## The 2x2 plot

After scoring all suppliers, plot them on a 2x2 with **Capability** on the Y-axis and **Market presence** on the X-axis. Use bubble size to represent inverse risk (bigger bubble = lower risk).

| | **Low Market Presence** | **High Market Presence** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **High Capability** | Specialists — keep on a watchlist for niche needs | **Strategic Partners — your primary panel** |
| **Low Capability** | De-prioritize | Incumbents — challenge or rationalize |

## How to use the output

The "Strategic Partners" quadrant becomes your primary supplier panel. The "Specialists" quadrant becomes your innovation watchlist. The "Incumbents" quadrant is your consolidation target. The "De-prioritize" quadrant exits the panel at the next contract review.

## Anti-patterns

Do not use this matrix for one-off transactional purchases. Do not score suppliers in isolation — always score relative to the specific business problem. Do not skip the risk axis to make the matrix simpler; risk is what separates a sourcing strategy from a procurement spreadsheet.
